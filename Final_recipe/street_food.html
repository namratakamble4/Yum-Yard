Recipes



Vada Pav

Known as the Indian burger, Vada Pav features a spicy potato fritter (vada) sandwiched between a soft bun (pav) and served with chutneys and fried green chili.

Pav Bhaji

A beloved Mumbai street food, Pav Bhaji is a spicy vegetable mash cooked with butter and served with soft buttered bread rolls (pav).

Misal

A traditional Maharashtrian dish, Misal is a spicy curry made from sprouted lentils, topped with farsan (crunchy snack mix), chopped onions, and served with pav (bread).

Dabeli

Originating from Gujarat, Dabeli is a spicy potato mixture stuffed in a bun, garnished with pomegranate seeds, peanuts, and sev, creating a sweet, spicy, and crunchy snack.

Bhel

A popular Indian snack, Bhel Puri is a mix of puffed rice, sev, vegetables, and tangy tamarind sauce, creating a crunchy and flavorful dish.

Pani Puri

A popular Indian street food, Pani Puri consists of crispy hollow puris filled with spicy, tangy water, tamarind chutney, and a mixture of chickpeas or potatoes.

Kachori

Kachori is a deep-fried pastry filled with a spiced mixture of lentils or peas, commonly served with chutneys or yogurt.

Samosa

A samosa is a popular Indian snack consisting of a crispy, deep-fried pastry filled with a spiced mixture of potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat, often served with chutneys.

Cooking Tips & Tricks

Master the art of cooking with these simple and useful tips.

  • 1. Keep Herbs Fresh

    Wrap herbs in a damp paper towel and store them in a zip-lock bag inside the fridge to keep them fresh longer.

  • 2. Avoid Sticky Pasta

    Add a dash of olive oil to your boiling water to prevent pasta from sticking together.

  • 3. Use Room Temperature Ingredients

    When baking, bring butter, eggs, and milk to room temperature to achieve the perfect texture.

  • 4. Taste as You Go

    Tasting your food throughout the cooking process ensures a balanced flavor in the final dish.

  • 5. Sharpen Your Knives

    A sharp knife makes prep work faster and safer. Sharpen your knives regularly for best results.

  • 6. Use Salt Generously

    Season each step of your cooking to layer flavors effectively, rather than adding salt only at the end.

  • 7. Let Meat Rest

    After cooking meat, let it rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute, making it more tender and flavorful.

  • 8. Don't Overcrowd the Pan

    Overcrowding the pan lowers the temperature and prevents proper browning. Cook in batches if necessary.

  • 9. Keep Your Cutting Board Steady

    Place a damp kitchen towel under your cutting board to keep it from sliding while you chop.
