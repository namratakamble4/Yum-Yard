Kanda-pohe

Kande pohe is a popular Indian breakfast dish made with flattened rice (poha), onions (kande), and a mix of spices, often garnished with peanuts, mustard seeds, and fresh coriander for added flavor and texture.

Upama

Upma is a savory South Indian dish made with semolina, vegetables, and spices, deliciously textured.

Sheera

Sheera is a sweet Indian dessert made from semolina, sugar, ghee, and flavored with cardamom and nuts.

Sandwich

A sandwich is a food item consisting of two slices of bread filled with various ingredients like meats, cheeses, vegetables, and spreads.

Aloo kulcha

Aloo kulcha is a stuffed Indian bread made with unleavened wheat flour and filled with spiced mashed potatoes, typically cooked in a tandoor or on a skillet until golden brown.

Kat-vada

Kat-Vada is a Maharashtrian dish featuring crispy lentil fritters (vada) served with a spicy, tangy lentil soup (sambar), often accompanied by coconut chutney.

Puri Bhaji

Puri bhaji is a popular Indian breakfast dish consisting of deep-fried unleavened bread (puri) served with a flavorful spiced potato curry (bhaji).

Kurdai chura

Kurdai is a traditional Maharashtrian dish made from soaked semolina (rava) shaped into thin discs, lightly spiced, and then tempered with mustard seeds and curry leaves for added flavor.

Cooking Tips & Tricks

Master the art of cooking with these simple and useful tips.

  • 1. Keep Herbs Fresh

    Wrap herbs in a damp paper towel and store them in a zip-lock bag inside the fridge to keep them fresh longer.

  • 2. Avoid Sticky Pasta

    Add a dash of olive oil to your boiling water to prevent pasta from sticking together.

  • 3. Use Room Temperature Ingredients

    When baking, bring butter, eggs, and milk to room temperature to achieve the perfect texture.

  • 4. Taste as You Go

    Tasting your food throughout the cooking process ensures a balanced flavor in the final dish.

  • 5. Sharpen Your Knives

    A sharp knife makes prep work faster and safer. Sharpen your knives regularly for best results.

  • 6. Use Salt Generously

    Season each step of your cooking to layer flavors effectively, rather than adding salt only at the end.

  • 7. Let Meat Rest

    After cooking meat, let it rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute, making it more tender and flavorful.

  • 8. Don't Overcrowd the Pan

    Overcrowding the pan lowers the temperature and prevents proper browning. Cook in batches if necessary.

  • 9. Keep Your Cutting Board Steady

    Place a damp kitchen towel under your cutting board to keep it from sliding while you chop.
